# 2025 AHA ECC CPR Guidelines — Key Changes for Course Content Update

Source: https://cpr.heart.org/en/resuscitation-science/cpr-and-ecc-guidelines/

---

## Part 7: Adult Basic Life Support (BLS) — Key 2025 Changes

### Age Definitions
- **Adult**: Puberty and older
- **Child**: 1 year to puberty
- **Infant**: Under 1 year
- **Neonate**: Birth to 28 days

### Compression-Only CPR (Hands-Only)
- Acceptable for adult OHCA by lay rescuers who are **unwilling or unable** to give breaths
- **NOT recommended** for infants and children — breaths are critical in paediatric arrest

### High-Quality CPR Parameters (2025 — unchanged from 2020)
- Rate: **100–120 compressions/min**
- Depth: **At least 2 inches (5 cm)** for adults; **at least 1/3 AP diameter** for children/infants
- Full chest recoil between compressions
- Compression fraction: **≥60%** (minimise interruptions)
- Ratio: **30:2** (1 or 2 rescuers without advanced airway); **continuous compressions + 10 breaths/min** with advanced airway

### AED
- Attach and use as soon as available
- Minimise peri-shock pauses — resume CPR immediately after shock

### 2025 NEW: Infant Compression Technique
- **2-finger technique along sternum ELIMINATED** — insufficient depth
- Recommended: **2 thumb-encircling hands** (preferred, 2 rescuers) OR **1-hand technique** (heel of one hand)
- If chest cannot be encircled: heel of 1 hand

### 2025 NEW: Choking (FBAO)
- **Infants**: 5 back blows + 5 **chest thrusts** (NOT abdominal thrusts)
- **Children**: 5 back blows + 5 abdominal thrusts
- **Adults**: Abdominal thrusts (Heimlich manoeuvre) — unchanged

### 2025 NEW: Ventilation Rate
- Infants/children with advanced airway or with pulse: **20–30 breaths/min**
- Adults with advanced airway: **10 breaths/min** (unchanged)

## Part 9: Adult Advanced Life Support (ACLS) — Key 2025 Changes

### Top 10 Take-Home Messages (2025)
1. **Rapid assessment of clinical stability** directs appropriate ALS treatment — poor organ perfusion = instability
2. **Higher first-shock energy (≥200 J)** preferred for cardioversion of AF and atrial flutter
3. **Updated TOR guidelines**: rule application based on EMS scope (BLS, ALS, or universal TOR rule); **ETCO₂ must NOT be used in isolation** to end resuscitative efforts
4. **Vector change (VC) and double sequential defibrillation (DSD)**: usefulness not established for shock-refractory VF; further investigation needed
5. **Head-up CPR**: discouraged outside rigorous clinical trials
6. **Intra-arrest medications via ETT removed** — replaced by IV/IO
7. **POCUS** by experienced professionals during arrest may be considered to diagnose reversible causes if CPR is not interrupted
8. **Polymorphic VT is always unstable** — treat immediately with defibrillation; delays worsen outcomes
9. **IV access remains first-line** for drug administration; **IO is reasonable alternative** if IV not feasible or delayed
10. **Arrhythmias can be both cause and manifestation of instability** — evaluate proximal cause

### Defibrillation (2025 Updates)
- **Biphasic**: Use manufacturer-recommended energy; if unknown, use maximum available
- **First shock for VF/pVT**: 200 J biphasic (or device-specific)
- **Cardioversion of AF/flutter**: ≥200 J preferred
- **Polymorphic VT**: Always treat with immediate defibrillation (never synchronised cardioversion)
- **DSD**: Not recommended as routine — no established benefit

### Vasopressors (2025 — unchanged core recommendation)
- **Epinephrine 1 mg IV/IO every 3–5 min** for non-shockable rhythms (PEA/asystole): give as soon as possible
- **Epinephrine for VF/pVT**: after 2nd shock (unchanged)
- **Vasopressin**: removed from algorithm (no added benefit over epinephrine)

### Antiarrhythmics (2025)
- **Amiodarone 300 mg IV/IO** (first dose) or **Lidocaine 1–1.5 mg/kg IV/IO** for shock-refractory VF/pVT
- Both remain acceptable; no clear superiority of one over the other
- Amiodarone second dose: 150 mg; Lidocaine second dose: 0.5–0.75 mg/kg

### Vascular Access
- **IV first**, IO second if IV not feasible
- Central line: not recommended as first-line during active CPR

### Advanced Airway
- **BVM ventilation vs advanced airway**: both acceptable during CPR
- If advanced airway placed: continuous compressions + 10 breaths/min
- **ETCO₂ ≥10 mmHg** during CPR suggests adequate perfusion; sudden rise to ≥40 mmHg suggests ROSC

### POCUS
- May be used by experienced providers to identify reversible causes (H's and T's)
- Must not interrupt CPR — use during pulse check pauses only

### Termination of Resuscitation (2025 Updated)
- Apply TOR rule appropriate to EMS scope (BLS TOR, ALS TOR, or UTOR)
- ETCO₂ alone must NOT be used to terminate efforts
- Consider transport if: shockable rhythm, ROSC at any point, or special circumstances (hypothermia, pregnancy, toxicology)

## Part 8: Pediatric Advanced Life Support (PALS) — Key 2025 Changes

### Top 10 Take-Home Messages (2025)
1. **Respiratory arrest is the primary cause** of cardiac arrest in infants and children — ventilation is critical
2. **Lay rescuers should provide breaths** for paediatric OHCA if able and willing — breaths + compressions improves survival
3. **Ventilation rate: 20–30 breaths/min** for infants/children with advanced airway or with pulse
4. **Infant FBAO**: 5 back blows + 5 chest thrusts (NOT abdominal thrusts)
5. **Child FBAO**: 5 back blows + 5 abdominal thrusts
6. **Unresponsive + abnormal/gasping breathing** = activate EMS + start CPR immediately
7. **High-quality CPR**: adequate rate and depth, minimal interruptions, full recoil, avoid excessive ventilation
8. **Infant compression**: 2 thumb-encircling hands preferred; 1-hand if chest cannot be encircled; **2-finger technique ELIMINATED**
9. **AED**: attach as soon as available with paediatric attenuator/pads if available
10. **Prompt defibrillation** for VF/pVT — minimise peri-shock pauses

### Epinephrine in Paediatric Arrest (2025 — unchanged)
- **0.01 mg/kg IV/IO** (1:10,000 solution) every 3–5 min
- Max single dose: 1 mg
- Give as early as possible for non-shockable rhythms

### Fluid Resuscitation (2025 — UPDATED)
- **Septic shock**: Initial bolus **10 mL/kg** (not 20 mL/kg) — FEAST trial evidence; reassess after each bolus
- **Hypovolaemic shock**: 20 mL/kg isotonic crystalloid bolus, reassess
- Avoid excessive fluid — titrate to clinical response

### Defibrillation (Paediatric, 2025 — unchanged)
- **First shock**: 2 J/kg
- **Second shock**: 4 J/kg
- **Subsequent shocks**: ≥4 J/kg (max 10 J/kg or adult dose)

### Post-Cardiac Arrest Care (2025 — UPDATED)
- **Targeted Temperature Management (TTM)**: 
  - For comatose survivors of IHCA or OHCA: maintain temperature 32–37.5°C for ≥24 hours
  - **Actively prevent fever** (>37.5°C) for at least 72 hours post-arrest
  - Strict normothermia acceptable if TTM not feasible
- **Glucose**: Avoid hypoglycaemia AND hyperglycaemia — target normoglycaemia
- **Haemodynamic targets**: MAP ≥50th percentile for age; avoid hypotension
- **Oxygen**: Titrate to SpO₂ 94–99%; avoid hyperoxia (PaO₂ >300 mmHg) and hypoxia

### Airway in PALS (2025)
- **Bag-mask ventilation** remains first-line for most paediatric arrests
- Supraglottic airways acceptable if BVM not effective
- ETT: use cuffed tubes in all ages (including neonates and infants) — cuffed preferred
- Confirm placement with waveform capnography

## Part 6: Pediatric Basic Life Support (PBLS) — Key 2025 Changes

### Top 10 Take-Home Messages (2025)
1. **Respiratory arrest is the primary cause** of cardiac arrest in infants and children — ventilation and oxygenation must be initiated quickly
2. **Lay rescuers should provide breaths** for OHCA in infants and children if able and willing — breaths + compressions improves survival
3. **Ventilation rate: 20–30 breaths/min** for infants and children with advanced airway in place OR with a pulse
4. **Infant FBAO**: 5 back blows alternating with 5 chest thrusts — **abdominal thrusts NOT recommended in infants**
5. **Child FBAO**: 5 back blows alternating with 5 abdominal thrusts
6. **Unresponsive + abnormal/gasping breathing** = activate EMS + start CPR with chest compressions immediately
7. **High-quality CPR**: adequate rate and depth, minimal interruptions, full chest recoil, avoid excessive ventilation
8. **Infant compression technique**: 1-hand technique OR 2 thumb-encircling hands technique. Heel-of-1-hand if chest cannot be encircled. **2-finger technique along sternum ELIMINATED** (ineffective depth)
9. **AED**: attach as soon as possible using pediatric attenuator and pediatric pads if available
10. **Prompt defibrillation** for VF/pVT — minimize peri-shock pauses

## Heartsaver (Lay Rescuer CPR/AED) — Key 2025 Changes

### Core Updates
- **Hands-only CPR** acceptable for adult OHCA by lay rescuers unwilling/unable to give breaths
- **Breaths recommended** for infants and children by lay rescuers who are willing and able
- **Infant compression**: 2 thumb-encircling hands preferred; 2-finger technique ELIMINATED
- **FBAO (infant)**: 5 back blows + 5 chest thrusts (NOT abdominal thrusts)
- **FBAO (child/adult)**: 5 back blows + 5 abdominal thrusts
- **AED**: Use as soon as available; use paediatric pads/attenuator for children under 8 years if available
- **Recovery position**: Place unresponsive breathing victim in recovery position while awaiting EMS

## Summary of Most Clinically Significant 2025 Changes

| Topic | 2020 Guideline | 2025 Update |
|---|---|---|
| Infant compression technique | 2-finger or 2-thumb | 2-finger ELIMINATED; 2-thumb or 1-hand only |
| Infant FBAO | Back blows + chest thrusts | Unchanged — abdominal thrusts still NOT for infants |
| Child FBAO | Abdominal thrusts | 5 back blows + 5 abdominal thrusts (clarified) |
| Paediatric ventilation rate (advanced airway) | Not specified clearly | 20–30 breaths/min |
| AF/flutter cardioversion energy | Not specified | ≥200 J preferred |
| Polymorphic VT | Synchronised cardioversion | Always defibrillation (never synchronised) |
| DSD for refractory VF | Emerging evidence | Not recommended as routine |
| Head-up CPR | Emerging evidence | Discouraged outside clinical trials |
| ETCO₂ for TOR | Could be used | Must NOT be used in isolation |
| Paediatric septic shock fluid | 20 mL/kg bolus | 10 mL/kg bolus (reassess after each) |
| TTM post-arrest (paeds) | 32–34°C or normothermia | 32–37.5°C; prevent fever >37.5°C for 72h |
| IO vs IV access | IO reasonable alternative | IV first; IO if IV not feasible or delayed |
| Intra-arrest ETT medications | Mentioned | Removed — IV/IO only |
