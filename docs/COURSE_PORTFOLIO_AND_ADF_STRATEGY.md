## 1. Purpose

This document defines **what we teach**, **how offerings fit together (MECE)**, **pricing and progression**, and **how growth and revenue align with the mission** to reduce **preventable harm** to sick children by spreading **decision-quality** and **pattern recognition** to every provider who touches a child.

---

## 2. North star (clinical)

**After completing the full individual-provider pathway**, a learner should be able to **identify and correct any common reversible contributor to paediatric cardiac arrest and peri-arrest states** appropriate to their scope—within **airway, breathing, circulation, disability, exposure, and context** (infection, metabolic, toxic, traumatic).

We do **not** use “levels” as a pedagogical gate. We use **exhaustive topic coverage** with **simple, memorable** delivery. Courses are **mutually exclusive by topic** (no duplicate didactic) and **collectively exhaustive** across the paediatric emergency and resuscitation scope we commit to.

## 4. MECE logic (why this portfolio)

- **BLS / ACLS / PALS** cover **structured, credential-style** pathways aligned with international *style* training while remaining **adapted** to LMIC deployment (cost, access, blended learning).
- **ADF micro-courses** cover **residual specificity**—the “long tail” of conditions and contexts that generic courses cannot exhaust—without forcing a rigid level hierarchy.
- Together, they address **knowledge**, **pattern recognition**, and **execution** (reinforced by **ResusGPS** at the point of care).

**Gap we avoid:** Claiming two different courses teach the *same* core objective without distinction. Each micro-course should have **one primary learning outcome** and a clear place in the map below.

## 5. Advanced Diploma in Fellowship (ADF) — structure

### 5.1 Positioning

- **ADF** is the **umbrella** for micro-courses and fellow identity—not a single LMS module.
- Learners may **choose order** by **what they see most** in practice (e.g. sepsis before rare metabolic emergencies), while marketing communicates a **vision** of completing the full map and becoming an **ADF Fellow**.
- **Renewal:** Micro-courses are designed for **re-certification ~every 2 years**, supporting a **recurring revenue** relationship without punitive lockouts.

### 5.2 “World Cup” progression (metaphor)

Use as **marketing narrative**, not a rigid LMS gate:

| Stage | Meaning (narrative) |
|-------|---------------------|
| **Continent qualifiers** | Entry micro-courses / first completions in chosen domains. |
| **Group stages** | Breadth across domains (airway, circulation, etc.). |
| **Knockouts** | Complex multi-system scenarios; integrated assessments. |
| **Finals** | Capstone: **systematic approach** + **paediatric cardiac arrest** integration. |

The metaphor motivates **completion** and **peer referral**; **technical implementation** may remain flexible (badges, certificates per micro-course, optional leaderboards).

---

## 6. Cross-cutting ADF modules (standalone)

These sit **under the fellowship** but are **not tied** to a single anatomic domain:

1. **Systematic approach to the seriously ill child** — structured assessment, prioritisation, escalation, communication, and safety.
2. **Paediatric cardiac arrest** — prevention, recognition, high-quality CPR, rhythm management, post-arrest care, team dynamics, and debrief (aligned with PALS/ILCOR spirit, locally adapted).

## 9. Domain C — Paediatric circulatory failure (illustrative taxonomy)

### 9.1 Hypovolaemic shock

- Paediatric hypovolaemic shock due to **severe bleeding**

### 9.2 Distributive shock

- Paediatric **septic shock**
- Paediatric **anaphylactic** shock
- Paediatric **neurogenic** shock

### 9.3 Cardiogenic shock

- Paediatric cardiogenic shock due to **arrhythmias**
- Paediatric cardiogenic shock due to **LV systolic dysfunction (e.g. DCM)**

- **Pulmonary embolism** in children (recognition, initial stabilisation, referral)
- **Cardiac tamponade** (recognition, bedside findings, referral)

## 13. Incentives & platform ties

| Incentive | Intent |
|-----------|--------|
| **ResusGPS access (e.g. 1 month per micro-course completed)** | Every course **simulates or points to** ResusGPS pathways so learners know how to use the tool in real shifts. |
| **Referral discount** | Next course discount when a referred colleague enrolls — grows **decentralised** competence. |
| **Certificates + verification** | Trust and shareability; PDFs carry brand and QR (see implementation). |
| **Feedback before certificate download** | Captures **per-course** improvement signals for faculty and product. |

---

## 14. Target learners

**Every healthcare provider** who interacts with sick children, **including students**, subject to scope-of-practice and local regulation. Copy should never promise independent practice beyond licensure.

---

## 15. Marketing principles

1. **Choice + vision:** Learners pick where to start; the **ADF Fellow** path is the **aspirational horizon**.  
2. **Affordable micro-pricing** lowers trial friction.  
3. **Community and metaphor** (World Cup) build **identity** without shaming slow progress.  
4. **Outcome honesty:** We measure **completion, feedback, and ResusGPS usage** before claiming mortality impact (see STRATEGIC_FOUNDATION §12).
