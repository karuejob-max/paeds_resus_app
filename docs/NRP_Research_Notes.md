# NRP Research Notes - 2025 AHA Guidelines

## Neonatal Resuscitation Algorithm (From AHA 2025)

### Pre-Birth Preparation
- Antenatal counseling
- Team briefing
- Equipment check

### At Birth - Initial Assessment (within 1 minute)
**Decision Point 1:** Term gestation? Good tone? Breathing or crying?

**If YES (vigorous term infant):**
- Skin to skin with parent
- Routine care
- Maintain normal temperature
- Ongoing evaluation

**If NO (needs intervention):**
- Warm and maintain normal temperature
- Dry
- Position airway
- Stimulate and clear airway if needed

### Decision Point 2: Apnea or gasping? HR <100/min?

**If NO:**
- Check for labored breathing or persistent cyanosis
- If labored breathing/cyanosis: Pulse oximeter, Oxygen if needed, Consider CPAP

**If YES (apnea/gasping or HR <100):**
- Ventilate (PPV)
- Pulse oximeter
- Consider cardiac monitor

### Key NRP Principles (from research)

1. **MR SOPA** - Corrective steps for ineffective PPV:
   - M = Mask adjustment
   - R = Reposition airway
   - S = Suction mouth and nose
   - O = Open mouth
   - P = Pressure increase
   - A = Alternative airway

2. **Epinephrine Dosing (Neonatal):**
   - IV/UVC: 0.01-0.03 mg/kg (1:10,000 concentration = 0.1 mg/mL)
   - Volume: 0.1-0.3 mL/kg
   - ETT: 0.05-0.1 mg/kg (higher dose for ETT route)
   - Volume: 0.5-1.0 mL/kg
   - Repeat every 3-5 minutes if HR remains <60/min
   - Follow with 0.5-1 mL saline flush

3. **Volume Expansion:**
   - Normal saline or O-negative blood
   - 10 mL/kg over 5-10 minutes
   - Consider if suspected blood loss or poor response to resuscitation

4. **Chest Compressions:**
   - Start if HR <60/min after 30 seconds of effective PPV
   - 3:1 ratio (3 compressions : 1 breath)
   - 120 events/minute (90 compressions + 30 breaths)
   - Depth: 1/3 AP diameter of chest

5. **Target SpO2 by Time After Birth:**
   - 1 min: 60-65%
   - 2 min: 65-70%
   - 3 min: 70-75%
   - 4 min: 75-80%
   - 5 min: 80-85%
   - 10 min: 85-95%

6. **Equipment Sizes by Weight:**
   - ETT size: (gestational age in weeks / 10) or (weight in kg + 6) / 2
   - Preterm <1kg: 2.5 mm ETT
   - 1-2 kg: 3.0 mm ETT
   - 2-3 kg: 3.5 mm ETT
   - >3 kg: 3.5-4.0 mm ETT

7. **UVC Insertion:**
   - Emergency access route
   - Insert 2-4 cm until blood return
   - Confirm position before medication administration

8. **Surfactant Administration (Preterm):**
   - Consider for preterm infants with respiratory distress
   - Dose varies by product (typically 100-200 mg/kg)
   - Given via ETT

## Key Differences from Pediatric Resuscitation

| Parameter | Neonatal | Pediatric |
|-----------|----------|-----------|
| Compression:Ventilation | 3:1 | 15:2 (2 rescuers) or 30:2 |
| Rate | 120 events/min | 100-120 compressions/min |
| Epinephrine dose | 0.01-0.03 mg/kg IV | 0.01 mg/kg IV |
| Primary focus | Ventilation | Compressions |
| Initial FiO2 | 21% (term), 21-30% (preterm) | 100% |
