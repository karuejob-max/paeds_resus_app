## Why



Too many children still die from **preventable emergencies** in Kenya's public hospitals. In many county and sub-county facilities, departments run with **roughly two nurses per shift** — enough to keep the ward running, but not enough for one responder to leave every bedside unattended during a code. When a child arrests or decompensates, the department still needs continuous coverage while the team activates.



Paeds Resus exists to reduce **preventable paediatric death and harm** by building **hospital-wide readiness**, not by selling training seats in isolation.



---



## What



**Paeds Resus Hospital Emergency Readiness System (ERS)** is a **hospital-wide Emergency Response System** with **paediatric priority framing**:



- One coordinated **Emergency Response Team (ERT)** model — nurse-led, **24/7**, with hospital-wide activation so teams across units respond together

- During a code: one nurse responds while a colleague keeps the department running; a second responder can activate hospital-wide coordination (e.g. notify other departments of Code Blue in Ward X)

- Bedside **ResusGPS** guidance for time-critical paediatric decisions

- **Care Signal** for honest near-miss and incident reporting — facility-level QI, not blame

- **Paeds Resus Limited** AHA-aligned training mesh (BLS, ACLS, PALS, NRP) mapped to identified gaps — not generic bulk certification

- **Institutional dashboard** for activation metrics, training coverage, equipment readiness, and improvement actions



This is **readiness infrastructure plus AHA-aligned training**, not an ACLS vendor catalogue or seat bundle.

## How



| Layer | What hospitals get |

|-------|-------------------|

| **ERT / ERS activation** | Nurse-led team roster, 24/7 coverage model, hospital-wide coordination across units |

| **Training mesh** | AHA-aligned BLS, ACLS, PALS, NRP cohorts mapped to role and gap — delivered by **Paeds Resus Limited** |

| **ResusGPS** | Structured ABCDE flows, CPR Clock, weight-based dosing at the bedside |

| **Care Signal** | Monthly QI reporting; institutional review workflow; action logs for system fixes |

| **Readiness audit** | Equipment and process checklist — oxygen, defibrillator pads, paeds sizes, crash cart, activation pathways |

| **Dashboard** | ERT activations, time to response, paeds-specific activations, training coverage, open equipment fixes |



**Partnership approach:** We start with a readiness conversation — scope, ERT design, and staffing reality — then phased implementation. Training and platform access follow identified gaps, not a large upfront seat purchase.



**Contrast with bulk certification:** Most sites sell bulk AHA certification. Emergency response often does not improve; hospitals see little lasting value because staff use certificates for job mobility. Paeds Resus focuses on **working Emergency Response Systems** plus training that supports the system.

## Strategy (internal — not for public pages)



**ERS-first vision:** Paeds Resus leads with institutional Emergency Readiness Systems — ERT design, ResusGPS, Care Signal, dashboard metrics, and equipment audits — with **AHA-aligned training as a supporting layer**, not the product headline.



| Principle | Implication |

|-----------|-------------|

| **ERS before seats** | Sales and onboarding conversations start with readiness assessment and ERT scope, not certificate volume |

| **AHA as ally** | Public copy rides on the AHA name — "AHA-aligned training **plus** institutional ERS/readiness." Do not publicly claim ERS without AHA or attack AHA |

| **Honest staffing** | ~2 nurses per department per shift is the realistic baseline; ERS coordinates teams across units rather than implying one nurse covers everything |

| **Evidence discipline** | Publish only supportable process metrics (activations, time to response, ROSC where documented); no mortality claims until governed evaluation |

| **Pilot learning** | Early facility pilots inform the model internally; site names and outcome claims stay out of public marketing until CEO approves |

## 90-day metrics framework



| Metric | Purpose |

|--------|---------|

| ERT activation count | Is the system being used? |

| Time to first responder | Response reliability |

| ROSC rate (where documented) | Resuscitation quality signal — not mortality |

| Paediatric activations | Priority population served |

| Care Signal submissions | QI culture and honest reporting |

| Equipment / readiness fixes closed | System improvements from audit |
