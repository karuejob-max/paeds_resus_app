## Why

Too many children still die from **preventable emergencies** in Kenya's public hospitals. In many county and sub-county facilities, the paediatric ward runs with **one or two nurses per shift**, often **without a dedicated children's wing**. When a child arrests or decompensates, that lone nurse cannot be everywhere — adult wards, maternity, neonatal, and outpatient still need coverage.

Paeds Resus exists to reduce **preventable paediatric death and harm** by building **hospital-wide readiness**, not by selling training seats in isolation.

---

## What

**Paeds Resus Hospital Emergency Readiness System (ERS)** is a **hospital-wide Emergency Response System** with **paediatric priority framing**:

- One coordinated **Emergency Response Team (ERT)** model — nurse-led, **24/7**, cross-covering adult, paediatric, and neonatal emergencies when staffing is thin
- Bedside **ResusGPS** guidance for time-critical paediatric decisions
- **Care Signal** for honest near-miss and incident reporting — facility-level QI, not blame
- **Paeds Resus Limited** training mesh (BLS, ACLS, PALS, NRP) aligned to identified gaps — not generic bulk certification
- **Institutional dashboard** for activation metrics, training coverage, equipment readiness, and improvement actions

This is **readiness infrastructure**, not an ACLS vendor catalogue.

## How

| Layer | What hospitals get |
|-------|-------------------|
| **ERT / ERS activation** | Nurse-led team roster, 24/7 coverage model, cross-unit response when one peds nurse is alone |
| **Training mesh** | BLS, ACLS, PALS, NRP cohorts mapped to role and gap — delivered by **Paeds Resus Limited** |
| **ResusGPS** | Structured ABCDE flows, CPR Clock, weight-based dosing at the bedside |
| **Care Signal** | Monthly QI reporting; institutional review workflow; action logs for system fixes |
| **Readiness audit** | Equipment and process checklist — oxygen, defibrillator pads, paeds sizes, crash cart, activation pathways |
| **Dashboard** | ERT activations, time to response, paeds-specific activations, training coverage, open equipment fixes |

**Give-before-you-take:** We start with readiness assessment, ERT design support, and pilot-friendly scope — not a large upfront seat purchase. Training and platform access follow identified gaps.

## Mathari early evidence (honest)

**Mathari Hospital** is an early nurse-only ERT pilot using the Paeds Resus readiness model.

| Signal | Status |
|--------|--------|
| **ROSC improvement** | Early positive signal — team reports improved return of spontaneous circulation in activations |
| **Mortality reduction** | **Not yet claimed** — 90-day pilot metrics under collection; mortality impact requires governed evaluation at ≥1 month follow-up |
| **ERT model** | Nurse-led, 24/7, hospital-wide with paediatric priority |

We publish only supportable claims. Clinical outcomes depend on facility context, staffing, and adherence to local protocols.

## 90-day metrics framework

| Metric | Purpose |
|--------|---------|
| ERT activation count | Is the system being used? |
| Time to first responder | Response reliability |
| ROSC rate (where documented) | Resuscitation quality signal — not mortality |
| Paediatric activations | Priority population served |
| Care Signal submissions | QI culture and honest reporting |
| Equipment / readiness fixes closed | System improvements from audit |
