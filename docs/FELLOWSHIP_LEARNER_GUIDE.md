## What the Paeds Resus Fellowship is

The Paeds Resus Fellowship is a single learning pathway on the Paeds Resus platform. It combines structured micro-courses, bedside practice with ResusGPS, and monthly Care Signal reporting so clinicians build habits that support safer paediatric emergency care — especially where children are most vulnerable.

Completing all three pillars earns the title **Paeds Resus Fellow** when platform launch readiness is enabled. Progress is tracked automatically.

## What it is not

- Not an AHA BLS, ACLS, or PALS certification (separate offerings from Paeds Resus Limited).
- Not a government medical licence, nursing council registration, or hospital privileging document.
- Not a replacement for facility protocols, clinical leadership, or local standard of care.

## Three pillars

1. **Courses** — Every fellowship pillar micro-course (Foundational and Advanced per condition). Summative exams confirm knowledge; ward checklists and LMIC callouts support local adaptation.
2. **ResusGPS practice** — ≥3 attributable cases per taught condition using ResusGPS at bedside.
3. **Care Signal consistency** — ≥1 qualifying staff report per calendar month (EAT), with grace/catch-up rules, for 24 consecutive qualifying months.

## Foundational and Advanced

Foundational courses cover core assessment and first-hour actions. Advanced tracks cover complications and escalation. Complete Foundational before Advanced unlocks. BLS/ACLS/PALS are optional for Fellowship.

## Progress and exams

- **Diagnostic** — gap-finding at course start.
- **Formative** — practice during modules.
- **Summative** — end-of-course knowledge checks (required to complete a course).
- **Complete pathway** — 100% on all three pillars.

## Paeds Resus Fellow title

Attests automated three-pillar completion on the platform — **not** bedside competence or licensure. See certificate disclaimer on all PDFs.

## Bedside use

Use ResusGPS during real cases; mark unavailable drugs/equipment; apply ward checklists; report in Care Signal for QI discipline.

## Report unsafe content

Use “Report unsafe content” in the course player or ResusGPS footer.
