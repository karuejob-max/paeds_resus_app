# Paeds Resus - World-Class Curriculum Development Plan

## Mission
Create the most comprehensive pediatric resuscitation curriculum the world has ever seen - exceeding Harvard Medical School, Johns Hopkins, and the best medical institutions globally.

## Phase 1: Curriculum Architecture

### Module Structure (Harvard Medical School Level)

#### BLS (Basic Life Support) - 40 hours
**Module 1: Foundations of Pediatric Physiology (8 hours)**
- Developmental anatomy and physiology
- Age-specific vital signs and normal parameters
- Pediatric airway anatomy and challenges
- Cardiovascular physiology in children
- Respiratory physiology and mechanics
- Neurological development and response

**Module 2: Recognition and Assessment (8 hours)**
- Rapid primary survey (ABCDE)
- Secondary survey protocols
- Vital signs interpretation by age
- Pediatric assessment triangle
- Recognition of shock states
- Respiratory distress vs. respiratory failure
- Altered mental status evaluation

**Module 3: Airway Management (8 hours)**
- Airway anatomy review
- Manual airway maneuvers
- Bag-mask ventilation technique
- Oral and nasal airway placement
- Laryngeal mask airway (LMA)
- Endotracheal intubation principles
- Difficult airway management
- Cricothyrotomy indications

**Module 4: Circulation and Chest Compressions (8 hours)**
- Cardiac physiology in children
- Compression technique by age
- Compression depth and rate
- Hand placement variations
- Mechanical compression devices
- Pulse checks and assessment
- Defibrillation principles
- Vascular access techniques

**Module 5: Practical Skills and Scenarios (8 hours)**
- High-fidelity simulation
- Scenario-based practice
- Team communication
- Stress management
- Decision-making under pressure
- Debriefing and reflection

#### ACLS (Advanced Cardiac Life Support) - 60 hours
**Module 1: Advanced Physiology (10 hours)**
- Cardiac electrophysiology
- Arrhythmia recognition and interpretation
- Metabolic derangements
- Acid-base disorders
- Electrolyte abnormalities
- Pharmacokinetics and pharmacodynamics

**Module 2: Arrhythmia Recognition (12 hours)**
- Normal sinus rhythm
- Bradyarrhythmias (sinus bradycardia, heart block, asystole)
- Tachyarrhythmias (SVT, atrial fibrillation, ventricular tachycardia, ventricular fibrillation)
- Pulseless electrical activity
- Artifact and technical issues
- Pediatric-specific arrhythmias

**Module 3: Pharmacology (12 hours)**
- Epinephrine (dosing, timing, routes)
- Amiodarone vs. Lidocaine
- Atropine and anticholinergics
- Vasopressors
- Inotropes
- Sedatives and paralytics
- Drug interactions
- Pediatric dosing calculations

**Module 4: Advanced Airway Management (12 hours)**
- Rapid sequence intubation
- Difficult airway algorithms
- Cricothyrotomy technique
- Surgical airway management
- Capnography interpretation
- Ventilator management
- Post-intubation care

**Module 5: Resuscitation Algorithms (10 hours)**
- Cardiac arrest algorithm
- Bradycardia algorithm
- Tachycardia algorithm
- Asystole management
- Pulseless electrical activity
- Shock management
- Post-resuscitation care

**Module 6: Advanced Scenarios (4 hours)**
- Complex multi-system cases
- Team leadership
- Resource management
- Ethical decision-making

#### PALS (Pediatric Advanced Life Support) - 80 hours
**Module 1: Pediatric Physiology Deep Dive (12 hours)**
- Developmental differences
- Thermoregulation
- Fluid and electrolyte balance
- Metabolic rate variations
- Organ system maturation
- Immunological development

**Module 2: Shock Recognition and Management (14 hours)**
- Hypovolemic shock
- Distributive shock (sepsis, anaphylaxis)
- Cardiogenic shock
- Obstructive shock
- Shock compensation and decompensation
- Fluid resuscitation strategies
- Vasopressor selection
- Lactate and perfusion markers

**Module 3: Respiratory Emergencies (14 hours)**
- Upper airway obstruction (croup, epiglottitis, foreign body)
- Lower airway obstruction (asthma, bronchiolitis)
- Pneumonia and pneumonitis
- Pulmonary edema
- Tension pneumothorax
- Flail chest
- Aspiration
- Mechanical ventilation strategies

**Module 4: Neurological Emergencies (14 hours)**
- Status epilepticus
- Increased intracranial pressure
- Stroke in children
- Traumatic brain injury
- Meningitis and encephalitis
- Hypoglycemia and hyperglycemia
- Altered mental status differential
- Sedation and analgesia

**Module 5: Metabolic and Toxicological Emergencies (12 hours)**
- Diabetic ketoacidosis
- Hypoglycemia
- Hyperammonemia
- Poisonings and overdoses
- Acid-base disorders
- Electrolyte emergencies
- Hypothermia and hyperthermia

**Module 6: Trauma Management (12 hours)**
- Trauma assessment
- Hemorrhage control
- Fracture management
- Head trauma
- Abdominal trauma
- Chest trauma
- Burn management
- Trauma team coordination

**Module 7: Special Situations (2 hours)**
- Drowning
- Choking
- Anaphylaxis
- Sepsis

## Phase 2: Content Development Standards

### Each Lesson Must Include:
1. **Learning Objectives** (Bloom's taxonomy - Remember, Understand, Apply, Analyze, Evaluate, Create)
2. **Pre-Assessment** (knowledge check before content)
3. **Core Content** (text, images, videos, interactive elements)
4. **Clinical Cases** (real-world scenarios with decision trees)
5. **Practical Skills** (video demonstrations, simulation guidance)
6. **Knowledge Checks** (formative assessment)
7. **Clinical Decision Support** (algorithms, flowcharts, decision trees)
8. **Evidence Base** (citations, guidelines, research)
9. **Post-Assessment** (summative evaluation)
10. **Reflection Prompts** (metacognitive engagement)

### Content Quality Standards:
- Evidence-based (citing current guidelines and research)
- Culturally adapted (African context, resource availability)
- Accessible (multiple learning modalities)
- Engaging (interactive, visual, practical)
- Current (updated quarterly with new evidence)
- Comprehensive (no gaps in knowledge)
- Challenging (exceeds medical school standards)
