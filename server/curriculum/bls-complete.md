## Module 1: BLS Foundations and Chain of Survival (8 hours)

### Lesson 1.1: Chain of Survival
**Duration:** 60 minutes | **Type:** Video + Interactive

The chain of survival represents the critical steps needed to save lives during cardiac emergencies.

**Five Links of the Chain:**

**1. Early Recognition and Call for Help**
- Recognize signs of cardiac emergency
- Call emergency services immediately (911 or local number)
- Provide location and patient information
- Follow dispatcher instructions

**2. Early CPR**
- Begin CPR immediately
- High-quality compressions and ventilations
- Minimize interruptions
- Continue until AED arrives or professional help takes over

**3. Early Defibrillation**
- Apply AED as soon as available
- Follow AED prompts
- Deliver shock if indicated
- Resume CPR immediately

**4. Early Advanced Care**
- Professional responders provide advanced interventions
- Medications
- Advanced airway management
- Transport to hospital

**5. Post-Resuscitation Care**
- Hospital-based care
- Targeted temperature management
- Optimization of organ function
- Rehabilitation and recovery

**Interactive Element:** Animated chain of survival showing how each link contributes to survival.

---

### Lesson 1.2: Recognition of Cardiac Arrest
**Duration:** 60 minutes | **Type:** Video + Assessment

Rapid recognition of cardiac arrest is critical for initiating the chain of survival.

**Signs of Cardiac Arrest:**
- Unresponsiveness (no response to stimulation)
- Abnormal or absent breathing
- No pulse (optional check for healthcare providers)

**Checking Responsiveness:**
- Tap shoulders and shout "Are you okay?"
- Look for response
- Check for 10 seconds maximum

**Checking Breathing:**
- Look for chest rise and fall
- Listen for breathing sounds
- Feel for air movement
- Check for 10 seconds maximum

**Pulse Check (Healthcare Providers Only):**
- Check carotid pulse (adults and children)
- Check brachial pulse (infants)
- Take 10 seconds maximum
- If unsure, assume cardiac arrest

**Assessment:** Learner must recognize cardiac arrest and initiate appropriate response.

---

### Lesson 1.3: Calling for Help
**Duration:** 60 minutes | **Type:** Video + Role-Play

Calling for help immediately is critical for accessing emergency services and AED.

**When to Call:**
- Immediately upon recognizing cardiac arrest
- Do not delay to check for pulse or breathing
- Call before starting CPR if alone

**What to Report:**
- Location (address, cross streets, landmarks)
- Patient condition (unresponsive, not breathing)
- Number of patients
- Any hazards
- Your name and callback number

**AED Request:**
- Ask dispatcher to send AED
- Ask bystanders to retrieve AED
- Do not delay CPR to find AED

**Role-Play:** Learner practices calling emergency services.

---

### Lesson 1.4: CPR Overview
**Duration:** 60 minutes | **Type:** Video + Interactive

CPR is the foundation of BLS and can maintain circulation until professional help arrives.

**CPR Components:**
- Chest compressions (circulation)
- Rescue breathing (oxygenation)
- Defibrillation (if indicated)

**CPR Effectiveness:**
- Maintains cerebral and coronary perfusion
- Buys time for professional interventions
- Improves survival when started early
- Survival decreases with each minute of delay

**Compression-Only CPR:**
- Effective for adults
- Easier to teach and perform
- May be preferred by untrained rescuers
- Provides adequate oxygenation for first few minutes

**CPR with Rescue Breathing:**
- More effective for children and infants
- Provides both circulation and oxygenation
- Requires training
- Preferred by healthcare providers

**Interactive Element:** CPR effectiveness simulator showing survival rates with different interventions.

### Lesson 1.5: Legal and Ethical Considerations
**Duration:** 60 minutes | **Type:** Video + Assessment

Understanding legal and ethical issues protects rescuers and respects patient wishes.

**Good Samaritan Laws:**
- Protect rescuers from liability
- Apply to trained and untrained rescuers
- Vary by jurisdiction
- Encourage bystander intervention

**Duty to Rescue:**
- Healthcare providers may have legal duty
- Varies by jurisdiction
- Professional responsibility
- Ethical obligation

**Do Not Resuscitate (DNR) Orders:**
- Respect patient wishes
- Look for DNR bracelet or documentation
- Clarify with family if uncertain
- Do not initiate CPR if valid DNR present

**Consent:**
- Implied consent in unconscious patients
- Respect patient autonomy
- Follow advance directives
- Communicate with family

**Assessment:** Learner must understand legal and ethical considerations in BLS.

## Module 2: Adult CPR (8 hours)

### Lesson 2.1: Adult CPR Technique
**Duration:** 60 minutes | **Type:** Video + Hands-On

Proper CPR technique maximizes effectiveness and minimizes complications.

**Patient Positioning:**
- Supine on firm surface
- Head, neck, and spine aligned
- Arms at sides
- Legs extended

**Rescuer Positioning:**
- Kneel beside patient
- Position chest over patient's chest
- Maintain upright posture
- Distribute weight evenly

**Hand Placement:**
- Place heel of one hand on center of chest (between nipples)
- Place heel of other hand on top
- Interlock fingers
- Keep arms straight
- Position shoulders directly over hands

**Compression Technique:**
- Push hard and fast
- Compress at least 2 inches (5 cm)
- Compress at rate of 100-120 compressions per minute
- Allow complete recoil between compressions
- Minimize interruptions

**Hands-On Practice:** Learner practices CPR on adult manikin with feedback.

---

### Lesson 2.2: Rescue Breathing Technique
**Duration:** 60 minutes | **Type:** Video + Hands-On

Rescue breathing provides oxygenation to support circulation.

**Airway Opening:**
- Head tilt-chin lift technique
- Tilt head back slightly
- Lift chin upward
- Open mouth

**Mouth-to-Mouth Breathing:**
- Take normal breath (not deep)
- Seal lips around patient's mouth
- Blow steadily for 1 second
- Watch for chest rise
- Release and allow exhalation

**Mouth-to-Nose Breathing:**
- Close patient's mouth
- Seal lips around nose
- Blow steadily for 1 second
- Watch for chest rise
- Release and allow exhalation

**Barrier Devices:**
- Face shield
- Pocket mask
- Bag-valve-mask (BVM)
- Reduce infection risk
- Improve seal and ventilation

**Hands-On Practice:** Learner practices rescue breathing on adult manikin.

---

### Lesson 2.3: CPR Cycles and Ratios
**Duration:** 60 minutes | **Type:** Video + Assessment

CPR cycles and compression-to-ventilation ratios guide the resuscitation process.

**CPR Cycles:**
- 30 compressions followed by 2 ventilations
- Repeat cycles continuously
- Minimize interruptions
- Continue until AED arrives or professional help takes over

**Compression-to-Ventilation Ratio:**
- 30:2 (30 compressions, 2 ventilations)
- Maintains circulation and oxygenation
- Minimizes interruptions
- Effective for single and dual rescuers

**Compression-Only CPR:**
- Continuous compressions without ventilations
- Effective for adults
- Easier to teach and perform
- May be preferred by untrained rescuers

**Switching Rescuers:**
- Switch every 2 minutes
- Minimize interruptions
- Maintain compression quality
- Prevent rescuer fatigue

**Assessment:** Learner must perform correct CPR cycles and ratios.

---

### Lesson 2.4: CPR Quality and Monitoring
**Duration:** 60 minutes | **Type:** Video + Assessment

High-quality CPR maximizes effectiveness and improves outcomes.

**Compression Quality:**
- Adequate depth (at least 2 inches)
- Adequate rate (100-120 compressions per minute)
- Complete recoil between compressions
- Minimal interruptions

**Ventilation Quality:**
- Adequate volume (visible chest rise)
- Adequate rate (2 ventilations per 30 compressions)
- Avoid excessive ventilation
- Maintain airway patency

**Monitoring Effectiveness:**
- Feel for pulse during compressions
- Look for chest rise with ventilations
- Listen for breathing
- Watch for spontaneous movement

**Signs of Effective CPR:**
- Palpable pulse during compressions
- Visible chest rise with ventilations
- Pupil response
- Return of spontaneous circulation (ROSC)

**Assessment:** Learner must assess CPR quality and make adjustments.

---

### Lesson 2.5: Special Considerations in Adult CPR
**Duration:** 60 minutes | **Type:** Video + Case Study

Special situations require modifications to standard CPR.

**Obese Patients:**
- May need additional rescuers
- Hand placement same
- Compression depth may be difficult
- Consider alternative positioning

**Pregnancy:**
- Displace uterus to left (left uterine displacement)
- Standard CPR otherwise
- Consider perimortem cesarean if no ROSC after 4 minutes

**Trauma:**
- Maintain cervical spine precautions
- Modified airway opening (jaw thrust)
- Assess for life-threatening injuries
- Treat reversible causes

**Hypothermia:**
- CPR may be less effective
- Medications spaced further apart
- Continue resuscitation until warm
- "Not dead until warm and dead"

**Case Study:** Special CPR scenario. Learner must adapt technique appropriately.

## Module 3: Automated External Defibrillation (8 hours)

### Lesson 3.1: AED Overview and Function
**Duration:** 60 minutes | **Type:** Video + Interactive

AEDs are critical tools for treating life-threatening arrhythmias.

**AED Function:**
- Analyzes cardiac rhythm
- Determines if shock is needed
- Delivers shock if indicated
- Guides rescuer through process

**Shockable Rhythms:**
- Ventricular fibrillation (VF)
- Pulseless ventricular tachycardia (VT)
- These rhythms respond to defibrillation

**Non-Shockable Rhythms:**
- Asystole (flat line)
- Pulseless electrical activity (PEA)
- These rhythms do not respond to defibrillation

**AED Advantages:**
- Automated analysis
- Voice prompts guide rescuer
- Minimal training required
- Effective for VF and VT

**Interactive Element:** AED function simulator showing rhythm analysis and shock delivery.

---

### Lesson 3.2: AED Operation
**Duration:** 60 minutes | **Type:** Video + Hands-On

Proper AED operation is critical for effective defibrillation.

**AED Setup:**
- Turn on AED
- Follow voice prompts
- Expose patient's chest
- Dry chest if wet
- Remove medication patches if present

**Pad Placement:**
- One pad on right chest (below right clavicle)
- One pad on left chest (mid-axillary line, 5th intercostal space)
- Ensure good contact
- Follow AED instructions

**Shock Delivery:**
- Ensure no one is touching patient
- Announce "Clear"
- Deliver shock
- Resume CPR immediately

**Hands-On Practice:** Learner practices AED operation on manikin.

---

### Lesson 3.3: AED Maintenance and Troubleshooting
**Duration:** 60 minutes | **Type:** Video + Assessment

Proper AED maintenance ensures reliability when needed.

**Maintenance:**
- Check battery status regularly
- Check pad expiration dates
- Ensure pads are properly stored
- Keep AED in accessible location
- Test regularly per manufacturer instructions

**Common Issues:**
- Dead battery
- Expired pads
- Wet environment
- Hairy chest
- Medication patches

**Troubleshooting:**
- Replace battery if needed
- Replace pads if expired
- Dry chest if wet
- Shave chest hair if needed
- Remove medication patches

**Assessment:** Learner must identify AED maintenance needs and troubleshoot common issues.

---

### Lesson 3.4: AED in Special Situations
**Duration:** 60 minutes | **Type:** Video + Case Study

Special situations require modifications to standard AED use.

**Children:**
- Use pediatric pads if available
- Reduces energy delivery appropriately
- Use adult pads if pediatric unavailable

**Implanted Devices:**
- Pacemakers or defibrillators
- Avoid placing pads directly over device
- Offset pad placement if needed
- Device will not prevent defibrillation

**Medication Patches:**
- Remove patch before placing pads
- Avoid contact with medication
- Wipe chest if needed
- Place pads in different location

**Wet Environment:**
- Dry chest if possible
- Can use AED in wet environment
- Ensure good pad contact
- Avoid standing in water

**Case Study:** AED use in special situation. Learner must adapt appropriately.

---

### Lesson 3.5: Integration of CPR and AED
**Duration:** 60 minutes | **Type:** Video + Simulation

Integrating CPR and AED maximizes effectiveness.

**CPR-AED Sequence:**
1. Recognize cardiac arrest
2. Call for help
3. Begin CPR
4. Retrieve AED
5. Apply pads
6. Allow AED to analyze
7. Deliver shock if indicated
8. Resume CPR immediately
9. Continue until professional help arrives

**Minimizing Interruptions:**
- Continue CPR while AED is being retrieved
- Minimize time between compressions and shock
- Resume CPR immediately after shock
- Avoid prolonged analysis time

**Simulation:** Learner participates in full CPR-AED scenario.

## Module 4: Choking Relief (8 hours)

### Lesson 4.1: Choking Recognition
**Duration:** 60 minutes | **Type:** Video + Assessment

Recognizing choking is critical for providing timely intervention.

**Signs of Choking:**
- Inability to speak or cry
- Weak or absent cough
- Difficulty breathing or stridor
- Inability to swallow
- Skin color changes (cyanosis)
- Loss of consciousness

**Partial vs. Complete Obstruction:**
- **Partial Obstruction:** Can cough, speak, or breathe (though difficulty)
- **Complete Obstruction:** Cannot cough, speak, or breathe

**Response to Choking:**
- Ask "Are you choking?"
- If yes and cannot cough/speak: Provide relief
- If no or can cough/speak: Encourage coughing, do not interfere

**Assessment:** Learner must recognize choking and determine appropriate response.

---

### Lesson 4.2: Abdominal Thrusts (Heimlich Maneuver)
**Duration:** 60 minutes | **Type:** Video + Hands-On

Abdominal thrusts are the primary technique for relieving choking in conscious adults and children.

**Technique:**
1. Stand behind patient
2. Wrap arms around waist
3. Place fist just above navel, below ribcage
4. Grasp fist with other hand
5. Press fist into abdomen with quick, upward thrusts
6. Repeat until object is expelled or patient becomes unconscious

**Positioning:**
- Stand behind patient
- Feet shoulder-width apart
- Maintain balance
- Use body weight, not just arm strength

**Effectiveness:**
- Creates sudden increase in intra-abdominal pressure
- Pushes diaphragm upward
- Expels air from lungs
- Dislodges foreign object

**Hands-On Practice:** Learner practices abdominal thrusts on manikin.

---

### Lesson 4.3: Back Blows and Chest Thrusts
**Duration:** 60 minutes | **Type:** Video + Hands-On

Back blows and chest thrusts are alternative techniques for choking relief.

**Back Blows:**
1. Stand to side of patient
2. Support patient's chest with one hand
3. Lean patient forward
4. Deliver 5 sharp blows between shoulder blades
5. Check if object expelled

**Chest Thrusts:**
1. Stand behind patient
2. Wrap arms around chest
3. Place fist on lower sternum
4. Deliver 5 quick, forceful thrusts
5. Check if object expelled

**When to Use:**
- Pregnant women (chest thrusts instead of abdominal)
- Obese patients (chest thrusts may be more effective)
- Infants (back blows and chest thrusts, not abdominal)

**Hands-On Practice:** Learner practices back blows and chest thrusts.

---

### Lesson 4.4: Choking in Infants
**Duration:** 60 minutes | **Type:** Video + Hands-On

Choking in infants requires different techniques due to anatomical differences.

**Infant Choking Recognition:**
- Weak or absent cry
- Weak or absent cough
- Difficulty breathing
- Inability to swallow
- Loss of consciousness

**Back Blows:**
1. Support infant on forearm
2. Support infant's jaw and chest
3. Lean infant forward
4. Deliver 5 sharp blows between shoulder blades
5. Check if object expelled

**Chest Thrusts:**
1. Turn infant face-up
2. Support infant's head and neck
3. Place 2 fingers on lower sternum
4. Deliver 5 quick, forceful thrusts
5. Check if object expelled

**Do NOT Use Abdominal Thrusts:**
- Risk of internal organ damage
- Use back blows and chest thrusts instead

**Hands-On Practice:** Learner practices infant choking relief.

---

### Lesson 4.5: Choking with Loss of Consciousness
**Duration:** 60 minutes | **Type:** Video + Simulation

If choking patient becomes unconscious, CPR is initiated.

**Sequence:**
1. Call for help
2. Begin CPR
3. Each time you open airway to give rescue breathing, look for object
4. Remove object if visible
5. Continue CPR
6. Do not perform blind finger sweeps

**CPR Modifications:**
- Standard CPR technique
- Look for object each time airway is opened
- Remove object if visible
- Continue CPR until professional help arrives

**Simulation:** Learner participates in choking with loss of consciousness scenario.
